What's BIN/IIN?

BIN (Bank Identification Number) or IIN (Issuer Identification Number) is the first six/eight digits of a payment card (such as a credit or debit card). These digits identify the issuing bank or financial institution and help to prevent fraudulent transactions by verifying the authenticity of the card.

How to find your BIN/IIN?

If you're not sure where to find the BIN/IIN on your payment card, don't worry! The first six digits on the front of your card are the BIN/IIN. You can also check your card statement or contact your bank if you're still unsure.

What information does the BIN/IIN reveal?

The BIN/IIN provides information about the type of payment card (such as credit or debit), the issuing bank or financial institution, and the country where it was issued. This information can be useful for verifying the authenticity of a card and preventing fraudulent transactions.

Why is the BIN/IIN Lookup important?

The BIN/IIN Lookup is an important tool for preventing fraud and ensuring the security of payment card transactions. By verifying the authenticity of a card, merchants and financial institutions can reduce the risk of fraudulent activity and protect their customers' personal and financial information.
